
DISEASE:

pneumonia

CATEGORY:

chest_xray

ALIASES:

lung infection, bronchopneumonia, lobar pneumonia, community-acquired pneumonia (CAP), hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP), ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP), aspiration pneumonia

DESCRIPTION:

Pneumonia is an acute inflammatory condition of the lung parenchyma primarily affecting alveoli and small airways, caused by infectious agents (bacteria 50%, viruses 30%, fungi 10%, aspiration 10%) leading to fluid/pus consolidation, impaired gas exchange, and systemic inflammatory response; classified anatomically (lobar uniform vs bronchopneumonia patchy), acquisition (CAP outpatient, HAP nosocomial >48h admission, VAP intubated >48h), pathogen (typical S.pneumo lobar, atypical Mycoplasma interstitial "walking"). Pathophysiology: pathogen inhalation/aspiration/microaspiration → neutrophil influx/edema → consolidation hypoxia; S.pneumoniae capsule evades phagocytosis polysaccharide vaccine PCV20; COVID/SARS2 diffuse alveolar damage (DAD); Pseudomonas biofilm VAP. Incidence 250/10k adults/y US, 1M hospitalizations/50k deaths; mortality CAP 5-10% outpatient/20-30% ICU/50% septic. Risk CURB-65 score (confusion/urea>7/BPS<90/age>65/RR>30) predicts 30d mortality <1% 0pts vs 57% 5pts. CXR gold standard; blood/sputum culture guides de-escalation.

VISUAL APPEARANCE:

CXR consolidations: lobar right lower lobe (RLL opacity air bronchograms silhouette sign), bronchopneumonia multifocal patchy basal peribronchial thickening, interstitial atypical bilateral reticular Kerley lines, aspiration right upper lobe (RUL supine)/posterior segment RLL. Progression: air bronchograms patent bronchi contrast alveoli, bulging fissure expansion, cavitation necrotizing (Klebsiella/Staph), pleural effusion blunting costophrenic angle. HRCT ground glass opacities (GGO viral), tree-in-bud endobronchial spread, crazy paving COVID, reverse halo fungal. US B-lines consolidation hepatization shred sign. Resolution 4-12wk organized pneumonia; cavitation warns empyema/abscess. Mimics CHF (batwing perihilar), atelectasis volume loss.

TYPICAL LOCATIONS:

Lower lobes gravity-dependent (70% RLL bilat), RUL aspiration supine, lingula middle lobe poor drainage kids, right>left 2:1. Multilobar 30% severe.

SYMPTOMS:

Fever/chills rigors (90%), productive cough rust/gelatinous green sputum (S.pneumo), dyspnea tachypnea RR>30 (60%), pleuritic chest pain (40%), fatigue myalgia, elderly confusion/delirium (40%), GI nausea diarrhea atypical.

RISK FACTORS:

Age extremes <5/>65 (OR 5), smoking (OR 4), COPD/asthma (OR 4), diabetes/CHF (OR 3), immunosuppression HIV/chemo/transplant (OR 10), alcohol (OR 3), aspiration dysphagia/stroke, influenza/COVID antecedent.

CLINICAL SIGNS:

Crackles/bronchial breath sounds consolidation (80%), egophony "E>A", dullness percussion, tachypnea hypoxia SpO2<92%, sepsis SIRS criteria.

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS:

Acute respiratory consolidations clinical/radiographic/micro differentiates. CHF (bilat perihilar GGO Kerley B-lines BNP>400 cardiomegaly improves diuretic). Atelectasis (volume loss shift mediastinum no fever). Pulmonary embolism (Wedge infarct peripheral hypoxia no consolidation D-dimer/VQ). Lung cancer (solitary cavitate chronic weight loss). TB (apical cavitary upper lobe night sweats AFB). Organizing pneumonia (persistent migratory peripheral CT no pathogen steroid response). ARDS (diffuse bilateral GGO acute P/F<200). Fungal (endemic histo/blasto Southwest/Ohio valley subacute fatigue). Why detailed: focal consolidations fever sputum; pneumonia air bronchogram sputum culture positive vs CHF vascular pedicle widening B-lines BNP; atelectasis platypnea no leukocytosis; PE S1Q3T3 RV strain; cancer spiculated mass PET-avid; TB caseating granuloma Quantiferon; OP reverse halo traction bronchiectasis; ARDS non-cardiogenic refractory. Procalcitonin <0.25 viral vs >0.5 bacterial guides abx. CURB-65 vs PSI mortality. Blood culture 10% yield S.pneumo. Legionella U/A sens 80%. Sens CXR 70% early HRCT 95%.

SEVERITY AND RISK:

Medium/High risk (CAP 5-10% mortality outpatient/30% ICU; HAP 20-50%, VAP 40%).

RECOMMENDED ACTION:

CURB-65 0-1 outpatient amox-clav/doxy/azithro 5d, 2 macrolide+beta-lactam, ≥3 hospital ceftriaxone+azithro/vanc+zasythro MRSA Legionella; viral supportive Tamiflu<48h COVID Paxlovid; fungal ICU voriconazole/immunosuppressed; O2 SpO2<92%/NIV HFNC, steroids severe COVID/dex<7d, fluids cautious sepsis. Follow-up CXR 6-12wk high-risk.

COMMON MISTAKES:
